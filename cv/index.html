Scientific Experience__________________________________________________________

2015-Current: Institute for Applied Ecology New Zealand, AUT University, Auckland, New Zealand * Postdoctral research fellow 2009-2015: Extremophile Research Group, GNS Science, Wairakei, New Zealand * Conducted phylogenetic analyses to determine the boundaries of the novel bacterial phylum Armatimonadetes and closely-associated candidate division. Correlated the resulting clades with established taxonomic designations (Dunfield et al., AEM, 2012; Lee et al., AEM, 2013) * Assembled genome of Chthonomonas calidirosea, utilised primer-walking techniques to improve the assembly, and conducted genome analysis (Lee et al. ISMEJ, 2014) * Cultured C. calidirosea and conducted sugar-utilisation experiments with HPLC analysis * Extracted DNA from several recalcitrant C. calidirosea strains, performed mapping assemblies, and conducted a comparative genomic study (manuscript in revision) * Analysis of sequencing data from multiple platforms (Illumina, Ion Torrent, 454, and Sanger) * Processed and analysed sequencing data (16S rRNA genes and whole genome) and interacted with data archives (NCBI GenBank & EMBL-EBI) for data curation and submission * De novo & hybrid assembly (454 and Illumina) and characterisation of a novel group 4 thermophilic Acidobacteria sp. genome (manuscript in preparation) 2008-2009: Extremophile Research Group, GNS Science, Wairakei, New Zealand * Physiological characterisation of the novel species C. calidirosea, including a wide range of substrate utilisation and temperature/pH growth ranges (Lee, MSc. thesis, 2009, Lee et al., IJSEM, 2011) * Designed specialised PCR primers to assist in the detection and isolation of the candidate division OP10/Armatimonadetes in environmental samples and mixed cultures. 2004-2008: Internship programme and employment during undergraduate study * SAITL Dairy Laboratory - Industrial milk microbiology testing for antibiotic residue and microbial contaminants. * Honey Research Unit, University of Waikato - Analysis for honey purity using a viscometer, and honey-Staphlococcus aureus inhibition study with tetrazolium spectroscopic assay. * New Zealand Forest Research Institute Ltd. - Conducted qualitative reverse-transcription PCR to assess the expression of genetically-modified eukaryotic cell lines. * Diagnostic Medlab - Sample processing using microbiological techniques for the medical laboratory.
